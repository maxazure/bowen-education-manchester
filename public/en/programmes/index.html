Government Programmes

Bowen Government Partnership Programs Serving Community, Preserving Culture

Bowen Education Group actively participates in UK government partnership programs, dedicated to providing quality education and cultural services to the local community. We work closely with Manchester City Council, schools, and community organizations to deliver diverse community programs.

Through the government-funded HAF (Holiday Activities and Food) program, we provide holiday activities and nutritious meals for children and young people. Meanwhile, we organize cultural activities in Manchester's major parks, promoting Chinese-British cultural exchange and enriching community life.

Our programs not only provide children with opportunities to learn and grow, but also create platforms for families to participate in community activities and strengthen neighborhood relationships, contributing to building a harmonious and diverse community.

Government Partnership

Deep cooperation with Manchester City Council, with government funding and support ensuring program quality

Community Service

Reaching grassroots communities, providing free or low-cost quality services to families from diverse backgrounds

Cultural Exchange

Promoting Chinese-British cultural exchange, preserving Chinese culture, and enhancing multicultural understanding and integration

HAF Program

Holiday Activities & Food

Government-funded holiday activities and food program providing free holiday activities and nutritious meals for eligible children and young people, helping them enjoy meaningful and enriching holidays.

  • Fully government-funded, completely free
  • Nutritious and balanced meals provided
  • Rich cultural and artistic activities
  • Professional teaching team guidance

Park Activities

Community Cultural Events

Community cultural activities held in Manchester's major parks, including Chinese traditional culture displays, artistic performances, and parent-child interactive games, providing free cultural experiences for community residents.

  • Free and open to the community
  • Multicultural displays and exchanges
  • Parent-child interactions and family activities
  • Professional artistic performances
