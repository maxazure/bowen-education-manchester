What is HAF Programme

Holiday Activities and Food (HAF) programme is a key community initiative funded by the UK government, aiming to provide eligible children with healthy meals and engaging activities during school holidays. Bowen Group actively participates in this programme, offering diverse holiday activities to local children.

Programme Goals

HAF programme is committed to promoting children's comprehensive development through holiday activities. Our main goals include:

  • Healthy Food Security - Provide free healthy meals to eligible children, ensuring adequate nutrition during holidays
  • Physical & Mental Development - Promote children's comprehensive health development through diverse sports and cultural activities
  • Family Support - Provide holiday childcare support for working families, reducing family burden
  • Education & Entertainment - Organize various educational and recreational activities, allowing children to continue learning during holidays

Eligibility Criteria

HAF programme is for children and young people who meet the following criteria:

  • Age Requirement: 5-16 years old children and young people
  • Eligibility Criteria: Eligible for Free School Meals
  • Location Requirement: Residing in Trafford and surrounding areas

If you're not sure whether you're eligible, please feel free to contact us for consultation.

Programme Schedule

HAF programme operates during school holidays, with the following arrangements:

Easter Holiday (Typically 2 weeks)

Spring-themed activities, outdoor exploration, craft making and other exciting content.

Summer Holiday (Typically 4 weeks)

Summer camps, sports competitions, cultural experiences, arts & crafts and rich activities, making children's summer holidays fulfilling.

Christmas Holiday (Typically 1 week)

Festival celebrations, craft making, performance activities filled with holiday atmosphere.

Note: Specific activity dates will be determined according to local school holiday calendars. Registration information will be distributed through schools and parent groups.
